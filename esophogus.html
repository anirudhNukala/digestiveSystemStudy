Esophogus

The food is shaped by the tongue into a ball called a Bolus. After this, the food is pushed into the esophagus, which is a muscular tube that moves food from the pharynx to the stomach through a process called peristalsis. Peristalsis is alternating waves of contraction and relaxation of muscles that push food through the esophagus.
