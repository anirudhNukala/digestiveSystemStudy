Large Intestine

The large intestine includes the colon, cecum, and rectum. The cecum is important for fermenting ingested material. On the other hand, The colon leads to the rectum and anus with a major function of reabsorbing water from feces. The feces moves through the colon where if less water is reabsorbed it will result in diarrhea and if too much is reabsorbed it will result in the feces becoming too compacted.
